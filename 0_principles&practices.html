Philosophy Behind Skill-Ed:

Principles:

Skill-Ed as the name itself suggests is a course that teaches skills and processes. Here we try to master skills and understand processes instead of creating any single products altho products/ prototypes are a representation of our command on processes, each week brings new skills to master and challenges to deal with. Objective of all this is to orient students to an array of skills and teach them basics so they can get into depth of nay skills by themselves without any external assistance. Initial things I found out after joining this course as a student is to not overthink and start thinking from basics, fundamentals or first principles .Usually our brain complicates things without us being aware. Focus on the thing right in-front of us instead of worrying about future challenges. The goal is to understand processes by heart and learn skills, its a journey of self growth and progress, what students become after spending just a few weeks in the course is the Product(Core Objective) not just the stuff they’re trying to make.

Practices:

In order to achieve core objective of this course students must become instructors by the end of it. This is the most important practice which students have to follow through. For this very reason every student must document all of processes, steps, instructions, work, output as well as their shortcomings and mistakes so that next batch of future students can see and learn from their documentations. Objective of this is to make students observant, mindful, thoughtful, deliberate and make them understand value of giving back to community of creators and makers. This does not mean that they can’t make mistakes, its the other way around the more mistakes they make the better, but they still have to document it all so that others can see, learn and find how to avoid making same mistakes, or if they are stuck somewhere then how to troubleshoot, debug or rebuild it. This basically is a way of showing gratitude to the lineage of creators and makers who came before us and documented all processes, as a result of which things are relatively simpler for us to learn and to master.

Coming up with an idea:

First thing that we as a student have to do in the Skill-Ed program is to come up with a product idea.This could be done in two ways, first way is to identify problems, challenges in our surroundings and or our our own life and try to come up with a solution for it. The second way is to identify something that we are passionate about and try to create something related to it. Once we have a rough idea, we can start brainstorming and refining it by discussing it with our peers and instructors, researching similar products or solutions, and breaking down the idea into smaller parts or components. It is important to keep in mind that the idea does not have to be completely original, but should be unique in some way or provide a new perspective to an existing problem or need.

Learning and practicing skills:

Once we have a clear idea of what we want to create, we can start learning and practicing the skills that are needed to bring our idea to life. This could include skills like programming, 3D modeling, prototyping, or user testing, depending on the nature of the product. It is important to keep track of our progress and document each step of the process, including any mistakes or challenges we face, so that we can learn from them and improve our skills.

Collaborating and receiving feedback:

Throughout the Skill-Ed program, we will be working closely with our peers and instructors, collaborating on projects and providing feedback to each other. It is important to be open to feedback and constructive criticism, as it can help us improve our work and gain new insights. It is also important to communicate effectively and work well in a team, as many projects will require collaboration and coordination.

Continuous improvement:

Finally, the Skill-Ed program emphasizes the importance of continuous improvement and lifelong learning. Even after the program is over, students are encouraged to keep learning and practicing new skills, and to share their knowledge and experiences with others. This not only helps to maintain and improve their own skills, but also contributes to the community of makers and creators as a whole.
